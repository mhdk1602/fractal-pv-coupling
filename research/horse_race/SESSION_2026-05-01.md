## Statement, Defense, Evidence

This is the rhetorical structure to internalise before any cold
discussion of the work — preprint readers, conference Q&A, journal
correspondence, defence committee.

### The Statement

> Within-firm temporal coupling between the fractal scaling properties
> of price volatility and trading volume is a real, regime-dependent
> phenomenon across the full S&P 500. It does not, however, carry
> firm-specific incremental predictive content for any standard
> liquidity or volatility outcome under inference robust to firm-level
> dependence. Inference methods that condition on the time dimension
> instead detect a cross-firm time-period co-movement that the
> standard nine-benchmark liquidity toolkit fully absorbs in
> horse-race conditioning. The paper documents this descriptive
> phenomenon, the structural inference disagreement, and three
> transferable methodological observations.

The three methodological observations:

1. **Share-volume vs. dollar-volume Amihud differ in a multiplicative
   price-level factor.** Cluster-robust inference (including the wild
   cluster bootstrap) does not detect this confound. The diagnosis
   lives in the dependent-variable definition.
2. **Firm-conditional and time-conditional cluster-robust methods
   can disagree at the same data when residual dependence is strong
   on both dimensions.** The inference dimension reported should
   match the unit of forecast claimed.
3. **Increasing cluster count from G = 50 to G = 488 did NOT raise
   the Bell–McCaffrey CR2 Satterthwaite effective degrees of freedom**;
   it remained at approximately 1.0 because a small number of
   leverage-heterogeneous firms dominate the meat matrix. The
   Satterthwaite check should be reported as a routine diagnostic
   rather than assumed to scale with G.

### The Defense

The empirical claims rest on five layers of methodological discipline:

**Discipline 1 — pre-registration.** Phase 2 was executed under
`PHASE2_PROTOCOL.md` v1.0 committed to the public repository at master
HEAD `f8aa564` before any G > 50 data fetch. The protocol fixed in
advance: the universe (current April 2026 S&P 500 constituency), the
inclusion criteria (≥600 obs, ADF/KPSS stationarity, ≤5% missing
volume), the pipeline freeze (no code change between protocol freeze
and run that affects estimated coefficients or inference), the ten
target specifications, and a per-target decision rule with explicit
p-value thresholds for three outcome categories: A (predictive null
confirmed under all firm-conditional methods), B (combined-spec robust
signal under all methods), C (mixed). The protocol explicitly disallowed
adding new targets, changing window parameters, swapping inference
methods, or reframing a Category C outcome as Category B after the run.

**Discipline 2 — full inference menu.** Six baseline standard-error
specifications (HC1, firm-clustered, time-clustered, two-way clustered
with Cameron-Gelbach-Miller eigenvalue PSD adjustment, Newey-West,
Driscoll-Kraay) plus two small-sample corrections (Bell-McCaffrey CR2
with Satterthwaite df, wild cluster restricted bootstrap with
Rademacher weights and restricted-null residuals). This is the full
Cameron-Miller (2015) practitioner menu. Reporting all eight on every
target prevents cherry-picking.

**Discipline 3 — horse-race conditioning.** Following Goyenko-Holden-
Trzcinka (2009) §5.2, every focal-only specification is reported
alongside (a) per-benchmark regressions against each of the nine
standard liquidity benchmarks individually and (b) a combined
specification with all nine benchmarks plus Hurst controls. The horse
race against the standard toolkit is the binding test for
"incremental predictive content."

**Discipline 4 — estimator-independence verification.** Hurst exponents
are computed via two independent DFA-1 implementations (this paper's
wrapper plus the open-source nolds reference) plus the Lo-style R/S
estimator. Cross-estimator rank correlations confirm that H1, H2, and
H3 do not depend on the choice of DFA versus its alternatives. The
documented R/S finite-sample downward bias (Lo 1991) accounts for
level differences without affecting rank order.

**Discipline 5 — full-scale reproducibility.** Code, data scripts,
manuscript source, pre-registration document, and full intermediate
result pickles are archived at the Zenodo concept DOI `10.5281/zenodo.19611543`
(always-latest) with version DOIs per release. The seven-stage
pipeline reproduces all reported numbers from Yahoo Finance OHLCV in
approximately 60–75 minutes of wall-clock on a single workstation.
Replication runs from the same commit produce identical numbers up to
the WCR bootstrap seed, which is fixed in the repository.

### The Evidence

The numbers that anchor the claims, organised by hypothesis:

**H1 (rejected): cross-sectional null.** Pearson correlation between
full-sample H_|r| and H_v in the cross-section is null in this
sample. Tested at both G = 50 and G = 488 with the same conclusion.

**H2 (confirmed): within-firm temporal coupling.**

- G = 488: mean within-firm r = 0.531, median 0.615, 92.7% of firms
  positive, 80.4% above r = 0.3, 64.5% above r = 0.5.
- Range: minimum -0.570, maximum 0.974.
- Sector universality: every one of the eleven GICS sectors has
  > 87% of firms positive. Mean by sector ranges from Energy 0.660
  (95.5% positive) to Utilities 0.412 (87.1% positive).
- Hurst-estimator robustness: DFA-1 vs nolds DFA-1 Spearman ρ = 0.93
  (abs returns) and 0.79 (volume); DFA-1 vs R/S ρ = 0.52 and 0.40.
  Cross-implementation agreement is high; cross-estimator rank order
  is preserved.

**H3 (confirmed in distribution shape): regime amplification of
heterogeneity.**

- Low-VIX regime mean = 0.567, median = 0.666, std = 0.344.
- Mid-VIX regime mean = 0.497, median = 0.566, std = 0.299.
- High-VIX regime mean = 0.563, median = 0.738, std = 0.450.
- Mann-Whitney high vs. low U = 135,542, p = 0.0019.
- Means are essentially identical across regimes; the Mann-Whitney
  test detects the distribution-shape shift, not a mean shift.

**H4 (rejected for firm-specific predictive content; cross-firm
time-period co-movement detected): structural inference disagreement.**

Primary outcome (forward dollar-volume Amihud illiquidity at h = 21,
G = 488, focal regression with Hurst controls):

| SE method | t | p |
|---|---|---|
| HC1 | -2.56 | 0.011 |
| Firm-clustered | -0.92 | 0.357 |
| Time-clustered | -2.61 | 0.011 |
| Two-way clustered | -0.93 | 0.356 |
| Newey-West | -1.48 | 0.138 |
| Driscoll-Kraay | -2.25 | 0.025 |
| CR2 | -0.91 | 0.527 (df_satt = 1.02) |
| WCR | — | 0.720 |

Combined horse-race specification: every focal-only signal in the
nine-target battery is fully absorbed. Cleanest case: forward
Corwin-Schultz spread, focal two-way t = +3.64 (p < 0.001) collapses
to combined t = +0.07 (p = 0.94), a four-orders-of-magnitude
coefficient drop.

**Aggregate-CII as a market-state object (Figure 10).** Cross-firm
mean CII at each rolling-Hurst date over 2018–2025 vs. VIX: Pearson
r = 0.057, Spearman ρ = 0.097 (p = 0.033). The cross-firm dispersion
of CII rises sharply during stress episodes (COVID-19, 2022 rate-hike
cycle), but the mean of the cross-section is only weakly aligned with
VIX level. The time-conditional inference disagreement is not the
trivial consequence of aggregate-CII tracking VIX.

**Methodological observation 1 evidence — share-volume vs.
dollar-volume Amihud.** Side-by-side comparison at G = 50 (Table 9
in the manuscript): identical regression with identical controls;
only the dependent-variable denominator differs.

| SE method | Share-vol t | p | Dollar-vol t | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| HC1 | 9.67 | <0.001 | 1.16 | 0.246 |
| Two-way clustered | 2.90 | 0.004 | 0.33 | 0.745 |
| WCR (combined) | — | 0.018 | — | 0.517 |
| CR2 (combined) | — | 0.066 | — | 0.507 |

The share-volume specification produces a robust positive result
that survives the wild cluster bootstrap. The dollar-volume
specification (the standard Amihud 2002 convention) is null
everywhere. The arithmetic decomposition $\widetilde{\mathrm{ILLIQ}}
= \mathrm{ILLIQ} \cdot P$ explains why.

**Methodological observation 2 evidence — firm-vs-time inference
disagreement.** Already laid out in the H4 table above. The
disagreement is not ad hoc: firm-conditional methods (firm-cluster,
two-way, CR2, WCR) all agree on null; time-conditional methods
(time-cluster, DK) all agree on significant. The disagreement
persists at the per-benchmark horse-race level (CII significant in
7 of 9 per-benchmark specs under DK, null in all 9 under two-way).

**Methodological observation 3 evidence — Satterthwaite df collapse.**

- At G = 50 (v1.0–v1.2 50-firm sample): df_satt approximately 6.
- At G = 488 (Phase 2 panel): df_satt approximately 1.0 for both
  Amihud and realised vol primary specifications.
- Cluster count rose 9.8×; effective df fell.
- Mechanism: the Satterthwaite df is governed by the second moments
  of cluster-level score contributions, not by cluster count. A
  small number of leverage-heterogeneous firms (typically illiquid
  small-cap or recent-IPO names with extreme Amihud values) dominate
  the meat matrix. CR2 has effectively no power at df = 1.0; the WCR
  bootstrap and firm-clustered CRVE are the binding firm-conditional
  inferences at this scale.

## Postscript: v1.4.1 patch (later in 2026-05-01)

After the v1.4.0 push, an external reviewer pointed out three precision issues:

1. The "for any standard liquidity or volatility outcome" phrasing in the abstract and the Statement section was an over-generalisation. The paper tests ten specific outcomes, not all possible ones. Tightened to "for any of the ten forward outcomes tested in this paper."

2. §3.4 (Coupling Metrics) defined static coupling across "all $i = 1, \ldots, 50$ firms," inherited from v1.0--v1.2. Updated to "all $i = 1, \ldots, 488$ firms in the Phase~2 panel."

3. §5.2 (H1) still reported the v1.0 50-firm cross-section ($r = -0.02$, $p = 0.88$) as "rejected." This was the most material gap. Recomputing the cross-sectional Pearson correlation at $G = 488$ from the full-sample DFA estimates produced in `run_hurst_robustness.py` returned $r = +0.183$ ($t = 4.11$, $p < 0.001$, $n = 488$); Spearman $\rho = 0.163$ ($p = 0.0003$).

The H1 narrative therefore needed reframing rather than rejection. The cross-sectional dependence is small but detectable at the broader sample size — knowing a firm's $H_{|r|}$ explains roughly 3.4% of the cross-sectional variation in its $H_v$. The within-firm temporal coupling (mean $r = 0.531$) is roughly threefold stronger and structurally different. The paper's substantive contribution is unchanged: the temporal object is what is interesting, not the cross-sectional level dependence.

The §5.1 baseline numbers were also updated to G=488: $H_{|r|}$ averages 0.796 ($\sigma = 0.066$), $H_v$ averages 0.879 ($\sigma = 0.073$). Almost identical to the v1.0 50-firm means (0.795 and 0.881), which is itself a verification that the universe expansion does not shift the baseline-Hurst story.

Conclusion language (former "H1 rejected") and the Static-Temporal Paradox subsection were rewritten to reflect the reframe. Cover letter unchanged at this revision (the cover letter language was already neutral on H1's specific value).

The substantive empirical payload of the paper is unchanged. v1.4.1 is a precision-and-consistency patch, not a numerical update.
