### 8. Phase 2 results

- **H2 confirmed at G = 488**: mean within-firm $r = 0.531$,
  92.7% positive, 80.4% above $r = 0.3$, present across all eleven
  GICS sectors (Energy 0.66 → Utilities 0.41).
- **H3 confirmed in a sharper form than the original 50-firm
  analysis**: Mann-Whitney high vs. low VIX $p = 0.0019$
  (more significant than at $G = 50$), but means are essentially
  identical across regimes; the test detects a distribution-shape
  shift (high-VIX has higher median 0.738 vs. low-VIX 0.666 and
  substantially larger dispersion 0.45 vs. 0.34). H3 narrative
  changed from "coupling intensifies in high VIX" to "coupling
  heterogeneity intensifies in high VIX."
- **Primary outcome (forward dollar-volume Amihud) Category C
  ("mixed inference"), not Category A**:
  - HC1 $t = -2.56$ ($p = 0.011$)
  - Firm-cluster $t = -0.92$ ($p = 0.357$) — null
  - Time-cluster $t = -2.61$ ($p = 0.011$) — significant
  - Two-way clustered $t = -0.93$ ($p = 0.356$) — null
  - Newey-West $t = -1.48$ ($p = 0.138$) — null
  - **Driscoll-Kraay $t = -2.25$ ($p = 0.025$) — significant**
  - CR2 $t = -0.91$ ($p = 0.527$, df_satt = 1.02) — null
  - WCR $p = 0.720$ — null
  - The disagreement is structural: firm-conditional methods
    (firm-cluster, two-way, CR2, WCR) all agree on null;
    time-conditional methods (time-cluster, DK) detect a
    significant cross-firm time-period co-movement.
- **Battery of 9 secondary outcomes**: 7 of 9 reach Category C,
  2 reach Category A. Pattern is uniform: 5 of 9 focal-only $t$'s
  cross 5% under two-way clustering, but every single one is
  fully absorbed in the combined horse-race specification.
  Cleanest illustration: forward Corwin-Schultz spread, focal
  $t = +3.64$ → combined $t = +0.07$.
- **Cluster heterogeneity diagnostic**: the Bell-McCaffrey CR2
  Satterthwaite effective df *collapsed* to ~1.0 at $G = 488$
  (was 4.4 at $G = 50$, despite the cluster count rising 9.8×).
  This is structural: a small number of leverage-heterogeneous
  firms dominate the meat matrix. CR2 has effectively no power
  at df=1.0; the WCR bootstrap and firm-clustered CRVE are the
  binding firm-conditional inferences at this scale.

### Lead worth flagging

The firm-cluster null + time-cluster significant pattern at $G = 488$
is the statistical signature of a *market-wide* signal rather than a
firm-specific one. A natural Paper 2 would aggregate CII
cross-sectionally into a market-time-series and test whether
$\overline{\text{CII}}_t$ correlates with market-wide liquidity stress
indicators (NY Fed CFSI, Pastor-Stambaugh liquidity factor,
BAA-Treasury spread, VIX). The Phase 2 panel data already supports a
quick pilot; this is left as a candidate for post-PhD execution.
