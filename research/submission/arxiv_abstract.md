We document a robust within-firm temporal coupling between the fractal scaling properties of price volatility and trading volume across 488 S&P 500 firms over 2015–2026. The Coupling Intensity Index (CII), defined as a rolling Pearson correlation between two rolling Hurst exponents, registers a mean within-firm coupling of 0.531 with 92.7% of firms positive and the pattern present across all eleven GICS sectors. Coupling heterogeneity rises sharply during high-VIX regimes (Mann–Whitney p = 0.0019). Under inference robust to firm-level dependence (firm-clustered, two-way clustered, Bell–McCaffrey CR2, wild cluster restricted bootstrap), CII has no incremental predictive content for forward Amihud illiquidity (dollar-volume convention), realised volatility, the Corwin–Schultz spread, the Roll spread, or six other liquidity proxies; every focal-only signal is fully absorbed by nine standard liquidity benchmarks in horse-race conditioning. Time-conditional inference (time-clustered, Driscoll–Kraay HAC) detects a significant cross-firm time-period co-movement. The paper documents three transferable methodological observations: a price-level confound in non-standard share-volume Amihud denominators, the firm-versus-time inference disagreement, and a Satterthwaite degrees-of-freedom collapse at G = 488. Pre-registered protocol; full replication materials at github.com/mhdk1602/fractal-pv-coupling.
